Feature panels

Feature panels act as a form of navigation, allowing you to promote content in an interactive and visual way.

On this page

Featured Content

  • Feature panels

They are predominantly for use on homepages or key landing pages. On rare occasions, they may add interest when used sparingly on a content page.

They do not provide detail, but act as a top-level overview, giving a taste of a theme or aspect they are promoting, providing links to further details. Text character length needs to be restricted in order to present well across various screen sizes.

Successful feature panels depend on sourcing suitable media, video and images, which need to be in certain aspcts ratios with consideration to where subject is focussed and grouped according according to our principles in creating a vertical rhythm.

Standard feature panel

These are used to make a strong marketing statement hightlighting key features on a landing page and allowing users to navigate to further details.

Other instances of the feature panels on this page show how they can also incorporate other media (video, loops, maps, sliders and search to support your content to add interest and interaction.

They rely strongly on quality images and videos and should not be used unless quality imagery and video can be supported.

If you need to elaborate on a specific topic, and editorial feature panel may be better.

Block heading

This displays the topic heading. To keep it looking smart and tight, use a short heading and try stick to two links on desktop. You'll need to create short titles - about 32 characters.

This can be positioned to the right of left of the panel. Place the heading with consideration to the subject of the panel image. You don't want to the block heading to cover a key part of the image.

When there are multiple panel features used on a page, it add interest to alternate the position of the block heading from right to left.

Block description text

Keep the block description text short and concise. You are only giving a blurb. Keep to 4 lines on desktop - about 210 characters.

The block heading acts as a link to more detail about the subject.

Panel image

A strong, impactful image (or video) makes a panel feature successful.

Use the best quality image you can get. Images should be in a ratio of 16:9 with a (minimum size of 1920x1080px).

Depending on the placement of the block heading (right or left), the subject of the image should be either horizontally centred or slightly shifted to the right of left.

Keeping key parts of the subject vertically centred will also help avoid unusual cropping on mobile sizes.

Find out more about selecting and preparing images and videos.

Overlay subtitle and description

You can use the subtitle text, which is overlaid on the image, to link to a specific element of the block heading feature. This appears diagonally opposite to the block heading position.

For example, if the feature is about "Research at Kent", the subtitle could display a specific research project and link to it (this includes a chevron).

Related links

These are links which appear below the panel linking to related content.

You can have 3 or 4 related links.

Looping background video

Use a few seconds of looping video, without audio, to create an interesting background to support the topic of the feature panel.

The video should not be jerky/flashy/irritating. Subtle movements, which provide a suprise are best. Be aware that video will be cropped above and below at different screen sizes.

Editorial feature panel

Use to give a short overview of a topic with slightly more detail that a standard feature panel.

It can be used within the flow of a content page to higlight a related topic or detail.

Try and keep the heading to a maximum of two lines on desktop (45 characters) . The description text can have a maxium of 400 characters.
